      06/01/2021 - Changes to published positive NHS test result figures for students

      The figures published on Monday 4 January showing the daily increase of positive NHS test results for students over the Christmas break, from Monday 21 December to Monday 4 January, have been corrected.

      04/11/2020 - Changes to reporting positive NHS test results for students

      We have changed how we report student cases due to improvements in how we receive test data. Initially we relied on students informing the University they had received a positive NHS COVID-19 test. We have been receiving Public Health Wales (PHW) data for students in our halls of residences with positive NHS test results since 7 October. This has allowed us to update student records directly from 2 November using PHW data. We have also chosen the optimum time of day to run reports to ensure all records are counted. This ensures that we have the best information to inform decisions to support the health and wellbeing of our students and staff, and to provide data comparable with other universities for national tracking and comparison purposes.

      Due to these changes we have reviewed previous positive NHS test results data for students and removed some duplication. As the data needed to be re-run to give this consistency, previously published positive NHS test results data for students has been re-issued. We are continuing to monitor all categories of isolation and to work very closely with the local health authorities.

      About our asymptomatic screening service

      We know how important it is to be vigilant about the number of coronavirus (COVID-19) cases in Cardiff. We have introduced a number of extra safety measures to help minimise the risk of coronavirus (COVID-19) in our community. Essential to this strategy is our decision to offer a programme of ongoing coronavirus (COVID-19) screening to all students and staff who are asymptomatic (showing no signs or symptoms of the virus).

      All students and staff who are asymptomatic will have the opportunity to take a saliva test shortly after arriving on campus. Anyone who is displaying symptoms, or who receives a positive or inconclusive test result through our screening service, is advised to self-isolate, inform the University they are doing so, and asked to arrange an NHS test. Find out more about our screening service.
